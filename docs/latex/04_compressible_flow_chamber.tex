\section{Compressible Flow \& Chamber Pressure Solution}

\subsection{Introduction}
This chapter describes the physics of compressible flow within the rocket engine and the numerical algorithm used to solve for the steady-state chamber pressure. The core principle is the balance of mass flow supplied by the injectors and the mass flow demanded by the choked throat.

\subsection{Nomenclature}
\begin{description}
    \item[\(P_c\)] Chamber pressure [\si{Pa}]
    \item[\(\mdot_{supply}\)] Mass flow rate from injectors [\si{kg/s}]
    \item[\(\mdot_{demand}\)] Mass flow rate through throat [\si{kg/s}]
    \item[\(A^*\)] Throat area [\si{m^2}]
    \item[\(\gamma\)] Specific heat ratio [dimensionless]
    \item[\(R\)] Gas constant [\si{J/(kg.K)}]
    \item[\(T_c\)] Chamber temperature [\si{K}]
    \item[\(M\)] Mach number [dimensionless]
\end{description}

\subsection{Supply-Demand Balance}
The steady-state chamber pressure \(P_c\) is found by solving the residual equation:
\begin{equation}
f(P_c) = \mdot_{supply}(P_c) - \mdot_{demand}(P_c) = 0
\end{equation}
\coderef{engine/core/chamber_solver.py}{209}

\subsubsection{Supply Side}
The supply mass flow \(\mdot_{supply}\) is the sum of oxidizer and fuel mass flows, which depend on the pressure drop across the injector:
\begin{equation}
\mdot_{supply} = \mdot_O(P_{tank,O} - P_c) + \mdot_F(P_{tank,F} - P_c)
\end{equation}
\coderef{engine/core/chamber_solver.py}{101}

\subsubsection{Demand Side}
The demand mass flow \(\mdot_{demand}\) is determined by the choked flow condition at the throat:
\begin{equation}
\mdot_{demand} = \frac{P_c A^*}{c^*}
\end{equation}
where \(c^*\) is the actual characteristic velocity accounting for efficiency.
\coderef{engine/core/chamber_solver.py}{200}

\subsection{Isentropic Flow Relations}

\subsubsection{Throat Conditions}
At the throat, the flow is always sonic (\(M=1.0\)). The critical ratios are:
\begin{equation}
\frac{P^*}{P_c} = \left( \frac{2}{\gamma+1} \right)^{\frac{\gamma}{\gamma-1}}
\end{equation}
\begin{equation}
\frac{T^*}{T_c} = \frac{2}{\gamma+1}
\end{equation}
\coderef{engine/core/chamber_physics_fixed.py}{66, 71}

The sonic velocity at the throat is:
\begin{equation}
a^* = \sqrt{\gamma R T^*}
\end{equation}
\coderef{engine/core/chamber_physics_fixed.py}{76}

\subsubsection{General Isentropic Ratios}
For any Mach number \(M\), the local properties are related to stagnation properties (\(P_c, T_c\)) by:
\begin{equation}
\frac{P}{P_c} = \left( 1 + \frac{\gamma-1}{2} M^2 \right)^{-\frac{\gamma}{\gamma-1}}
\end{equation}
\begin{equation}
\frac{T}{T_c} = \left( 1 + \frac{\gamma-1}{2} M^2 \right)^{-1}
\end{equation}
\coderef{engine/core/chamber_physics_fixed.py}{274, 279}

\subsection{Area-Mach Relation}
The relationship between the local area \(A\) and the sonic throat area \(A^*\) is:
\begin{equation}
\frac{A}{A^*} = \frac{1}{M} \left[ \frac{2}{\gamma+1} \left( 1 + \frac{\gamma-1}{2} M^2 \right) \right]^{\frac{\gamma+1}{2(\gamma-1)}}
\end{equation}
\coderef{engine/core/mach_solver.py}{37}

\subsection{Numerical Solution}
The chamber pressure is solved using bracketed root-finding algorithms (e.g., Brent's method). The search space is bounded by:
\begin{equation}
P_{c,min} \le P_c \le \min(P_{tank,O}, P_{tank,F}) \times (1 - \text{margin})
\end{equation}
\coderef{engine/core/chamber_solver.py}{252}
